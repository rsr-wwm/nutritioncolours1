Pharmacological Profile: Saffron (Kesar)

Crocus sativus

Primary Mechanism of Action

Protects neural cells from oxidative damage and supports healthy neurotransmitter balances.

From a biochemical standpoint, the therapeutic properties of Saffron (Kesar) are attributed to its complex matrix of active botanical compounds. These phytochemicals interact with specific cellular receptor systems, helping to regulate physiological pathways, downregulate markers of chronic systemic inflammation, and support tissue recovery. By incorporating these compounds into circadian-aligned dietary patterns, patients can enhance functional enzyme pathways and promote metabolic recovery.

Active Phytochemical Compounds

🧬 Crocin 🧬 Crocetin 🧬 Safranal

Phytochemical analysis indicates that these primary active compounds operate synergistically to modulate cellular stress responses and promote healthy enzyme function. Supporting evidence shows that maintaining the integrity of these volatile molecules during preparation maximizes their bioavailability.

Clinical Dosage Guidelines

Consult a clinical specialist for personalized dosage targets based on biomarker analysis.

Toxicology & Precautions

Maintain safety by not exceeding recommended dosing, and verify organ clearance capability (kidney/liver) with a clinician.

Clinical Contraindications

  • Extremely high doses (>5g) can cause uterine contraction and toxicity; avoid massive quantities in pregnancy
