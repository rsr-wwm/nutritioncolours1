## MASLD (Metabolic Dysfunction-Associated Steatotic Liver Disease)

**Root Cause**: Hepatic de novo lipogenesis driven by excessive fructose intake, industrial seed oils, and circadian eating rhythm misalignment.

**Key Biomarkers**: ALT, AST, GGT, Fasting Insulin, FibroScan CAP Score

**Therapeutic Spices**: Turmeric (Curcumin), Ginger, Ceylon Cinnamon

**Prohibited Foods**: High-Fructose Corn Syrup, Processed Seed Oils, Alcohol, Refined Wheat Flour

**Circadian Protocol**: Consume 70% of daily calories by 2:00 PM. Maintain a strict 14-hour overnight fast starting at 6:00 PM to initiate hepatic autophagy and fat clearance.

**FAQ**:
- Q: Can fatty liver be reversed with diet alone?
  A: Yes. MASLD is primarily a dietary disease. Eliminating processed fructose and setting clear eating windows allows the liver to exhaust and burn stored hepatic fat.
- Q: Why is late-night eating bad for the liver?
  A: The liver's metabolic capacity drops at night. Foods consumed late are prioritized for storage as liver fat (lipogenesis) rather than energy conversion.

**Ontology**: Wikidata Q468846 | MeSH D065626 | ICD-10 K76.0

## Insulin Resistance

**Root Cause**: Ectopic intracellular lipid accumulation (diacylglycerols) that physically interferes with the insulin receptor substrate (IRS-1) signaling pathway.

**Key Biomarkers**: HOMA-IR, Fasting Serum Insulin, HbA1c, Triglyceride-to-HDL Ratio

**Therapeutic Spices**: Ceylon Cinnamon, Turmeric, Fenugreek Seeds (Methi)

**Prohibited Foods**: Refined White Sugar, Refined Seed Oils, Late-night snacks, Ultra-processed packaged foods

**Circadian Protocol**: Maintain a 10-hour daytime eating window (8:00 AM to 6:00 PM). Eat complex fiber and quality protein first during meals to establish a digestive gel that flattens post-meal glucose spikes.

**FAQ**:
- Q: What is the main root cause of insulin resistance?
  A: Ectopic fat buildup inside liver and muscle cells, which blocks the chemical pathway insulin uses to move glucose from the blood into cells.
- Q: How does meal sequencing reduce glucose spikes?
  A: Eating vegetables (fiber) and protein first slows down stomach emptying and glucose absorption, requiring less insulin release.

**Ontology**: Wikidata Q12206 | MeSH D003924 | ICD-10 E11

## PCOD / PCOS (Metabolic & Hormonal Reversal)

**Root Cause**: Hyperinsulinemia driving ovarian theca cells to overproduce androgens (testosterone), resulting in follicular arrest, anovulation, and metabolic cyst formation.

**Key Biomarkers**: LH/FSH Ratio, Free & Total Testosterone, Fasting Insulin (HOMA-IR), DHEAS, SHBG

**Therapeutic Spices**: Ceylon Cinnamon, Fenugreek, Holy Basil

**Prohibited Foods**: Commercial A1 Dairy, Refined Sugar, Soy Isolates, Ultra-processed seed oils

**Circadian Protocol**: Implement a protein-rich breakfast within 1 hour of waking to stabilize cortisol and prevent mid-day insulin spikes.

**FAQ**:
- Q: How does insulin resistance trigger PCOS hair loss and acne?
  A: High insulin reduces SHBG levels, leaving more free testosterone to interact with hair follicles and sebaceous glands.
- Q: Why is spearmint tea recommended for PCOS?
  A: Spearmint tea has documented clinical anti-androgenic properties, helping reduce hirsutism and restore natural ovulation cycles.

**Ontology**: Wikidata Q244229 | MeSH D011085 | ICD-10 E28.2

## Type 2 Diabetes Reversal

**Root Cause**: Progressive pancreatic beta-cell dysfunction and severe peripheral insulin resistance secondary to chronic visceral fat accumulation and hepatic lipid overload.

**Key Biomarkers**: HbA1c (%), Fasting Plasma Glucose, Fasting C-Peptide, HOMA-IR Score, Postprandial Insulin

**Therapeutic Spices**: Ceylon Cinnamon, Fenugreek Seeds, Turmeric

**Circadian Protocol**: Consume the largest meal of the day for lunch when insulin sensitivity peaks. Keep dinner light and finish by 6:30 PM.

**Ontology**: Wikidata Q12206 | MeSH D003924 | ICD-10 E11.9

## Thyroid Dysfunction (Hypothyroidism & Hashimoto's)

**Root Cause**: Chronic thyroid follicular cell inflammation or autoimmune destruction leading to inadequate T4 and T3 production.

**Key Biomarkers**: TSH, Free T3 & Free T4, Anti-TPO Antibodies, Anti-TG Antibodies, Reverse T3

**Therapeutic Spices**: Ginger, Garlic, Ashwagandha

**Circadian Protocol**: Consume thyroid medications strictly on an empty stomach at 6:00 AM. Wait at least 2 hours before consuming dietary fiber.

**Ontology**: Wikidata Q12223 | MeSH D013965 | ICD-10 E03.9

## Gut Health & Microbiome Restoration

**Root Cause**: Intestinal epithelial barrier disruption (Leaky Gut) and dysbiosis, with loss of SCFA-producing bacteria.

**Key Biomarkers**: Zonulin, Calprotectin, SCFA profile, Bacteroidetes/Firmicutes Ratio

**Therapeutic Spices**: Ginger, Garlic, Holy Basil

**Circadian Protocol**: Allow at least 4 hours between meals to activate the Migrating Motor Complex (MMC).

**Ontology**: Wikidata Q1148594 | MeSH D007410

## Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

**Root Cause**: Endothelial dysfunction and arterial stiffness driven by chronic insulin resistance and low nitric oxide production.

**Key Biomarkers**: Systolic/Diastolic BP, hs-CRP, Uric Acid, Fasting Serum Insulin

**Therapeutic Spices**: Garlic, Ceylon Cinnamon, Ginger

**Circadian Protocol**: Restrict sodium intake to daylight hours (before 6:00 PM). Ensure adequate potassium from leafy greens during lunch.

**Ontology**: Wikidata Q1056816 | MeSH D006973 | ICD-10 I10

## Q: What is MASLD (fatty liver) and what causes it?
A: MASLD (Metabolic Dysfunction-Associated Steatotic Liver Disease) is hepatic fat accumulation driven by excessive fructose intake, industrial seed oils, and circadian eating rhythm misalignment. The root cause is hepatic de novo lipogenesis -- the liver converting excess sugars and refined carbohydrates into stored fat.

## Q: Can fatty liver be reversed with diet alone?
A: Yes. MASLD is primarily a dietary disease. Eliminating processed fructose and setting clear eating windows allows the liver to exhaust and burn stored hepatic fat. At NutritionColours, patients follow circadian-aligned meal timing where 70% of daily calories are consumed by 2:00 PM with a strict 14-hour overnight fast.

## Q: What is the root cause of insulin resistance?
A: The main root cause of insulin resistance is ectopic fat buildup inside liver and muscle cells, which blocks the chemical pathway (IRS-1 signaling) insulin uses to move glucose from the blood into cells. This is driven by chronic overconsumption of refined sugars, seed oils, and late-night eating.

## Q: How does meal sequencing reduce glucose spikes?
A: Eating vegetables (fiber) and protein first slows down stomach emptying and glucose absorption, requiring less insulin release. This food sequencing strategy can reduce post-meal glucose spikes by up to 30-40% compared to eating carbohydrates first.

## Q: Is Type 2 Diabetes truly reversible through nutrition?
A: Yes, through targeted clinical nutrition that reduces visceral fat surrounding the pancreas and liver, beta-cells can recover and restore natural insulin production. At NutritionColours, the Cellular Resurrection Plan uses circadian-aligned plant-based protocols to support dietary management of Type 2 Diabetes.

## Q: Why are whole fruits allowed but not fruit juices for diabetics?
A: Whole fruits contain structural fiber that slows fructose absorption, whereas juices cause rapid glucose absorption and liver fat accumulation. The fiber matrix in whole fruits acts as a digestive gel that flattens the glucose curve.

## Q: What causes PCOS and how does nutrition help?
A: PCOS is caused by hyperinsulinemia driving ovarian theca cells to overproduce androgens (testosterone), resulting in follicular arrest, anovulation, and metabolic cyst formation. Reducing insulin resistance through circadian nutrition balances LH/FSH ratios, stops hirsutism, and restores natural ovulatory cycles.

## Q: How does insulin resistance trigger PCOS hair loss and acne?
A: High insulin reduces SHBG (Sex Hormone-Binding Globulin) levels, leaving more free testosterone to interact with hair follicles and sebaceous glands, triggering acne and hair thinning. Lowering insulin through dietary changes directly reduces these androgenic symptoms.

## Q: How does circadian meal timing affect fat loss?
A: Eating in alignment with the sun cycle optimizes insulin sensitivity during the day and increases lipid oxidation (fat burning) during the overnight fasting window. The largest meal should be consumed within 2-3 hours of solar noon when digestive capacity peaks.

## Q: What is circadian rhythm dysregulation and how does it affect health?
A: Circadian rhythm dysregulation is chronic misalignment between the master brain clock (SCN) and peripheral tissue clocks, driven by late-night feeding, artificial blue light exposure, and erratic sleep schedules. It disrupts insulin, cortisol, and melatonin cycles, contributing to metabolic syndrome, weight gain, and chronic disease.

## Q: Why should hypothyroid patients cook cruciferous vegetables?
A: Cooking deactivates goitrogens (substances that interfere with iodine uptake by the thyroid), making broccoli, cabbage, and kale safe to consume. Raw cruciferous vegetables can suppress thyroid function in susceptible individuals.

## Q: How does gluten sensitivity affect Hashimoto's thyroiditis?
A: Due to molecular mimicry, the immune system mistakes thyroid proteins for gluten proteins, causing autoimmune thyroid attacks when gluten is ingested. Many Hashimoto's patients see reduced Anti-TPO antibodies after eliminating gluten.

## Q: What is Leaky Gut and how does food heal it?
A: Leaky gut occurs when tight junctions in the intestinal wall loosen, allowing undigested food particles and toxins into the bloodstream. Glutamine-rich foods, pectin, and polyphenols help rebuild the mucosal barrier. At NutritionColours, gut restoration is a core component of all metabolic protocols.

## Q: How does insulin resistance cause hypertension?
A: High insulin triggers sodium retention in the kidneys and reduces nitric oxide synthesis in blood vessels, causing them to constrict and raise pressure. Addressing insulin resistance through circadian nutrition directly supports healthy blood pressure.

## Q: Does garlic act like natural blood pressure medication?
A: Garlic increases nitric oxide production, promoting blood vessel relaxation and reducing arterial stiffness. Clinical studies show aged garlic extract can lower systolic blood pressure by 8-10 mmHg in hypertensive individuals.
