Pathophysiology of Chrono-Nutrition

Metabolic restoration requires moving away from calorie restriction toward strict meal timing. Human glycemic tolerance is heavily regulated by circadian clock genes, with insulin sensitivity peaking during early daylight hours and declining after sunset. By scheduling carbohydrate-rich ingredients (such as ancestral millets, jowar, and jaggery alternatives) before 2:00 PM and restricting evening intakes, our protocols help prevent overnight blood glucose spikes, lower fatty liver lipid accumulation, and support healthy cellular repair.

Always consult our clinical team or a registered dietitian to match these recipes with your specific metabolic biomarkers (including HbA1c, fasting insulin, and lipid panels) for safe, personalized health outcomes.
